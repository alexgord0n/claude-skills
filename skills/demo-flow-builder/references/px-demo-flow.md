# PX Demo Flow Reference

## Core Value Proposition
Gainsight PX gives product and CS teams visibility into what users are actually doing in your application, lets you engage them contextually and in real-time, and feeds that telemetry back to your CS team -- so you can improve onboarding, drive adoption, reduce churn risk, and scale without proportionally scaling headcount.

---

## Demo Sections (in suggested order)

### 1. End-User Experience (EmailMonkey framing)
**Value:** Show what the end-user actually experiences -- this is what your customers feel
- Login triggers attribute passing (CS + Salesforce)
- Everything tracked from first click
- Walk-through guide (images, video, GIFs, tooltips)
- NPS survey
- Dialog (webinar announcement, academy CTA)
- Multi-question form with branching logic
- Slider (new features)
- Paywall (trial users -- no dev resources required)
- Banner
- CES survey
- KCB: self-serve portal -- guides, articles, community, courses, federated search

**Core value hook:** "As soon as I log in, we start tracking -- and we can start guiding -- without any dev work from your team."

**KCB value:** Support ticket deflection, TTV reduction, onboarding at scale

---

### 2. Tag + Product Mapper
**Value:** No-code instrumentation -- product insights without engineering dependency
- Point-and-click tagging of UI elements
- 2 types: Features (events, clicks, page views) + Modules (folders/rollups)
- Hierarchy: organize by page, functional area, or full product
- Suggestion Mode: auto-creates hierarchy from URL structure and traffic
  - Scales with your app, picks up new URLs automatically
- Backfill: don't have to instrument on day 1
- Drag-and-drop organization

**Core value hook:** "This saves significant dev resources and reduces TTV for instrumentation -- your team can own this without engineering tickets."

---

### 3. Dashboards
**Value:** High-level visibility into key metrics, configurable by role and team
- 36 OOTB KPI widgets
- Frequency metrics (active users, sign-in trends)
- Feedback widgets (NPS, CES, effort scores)
- TTV reports (time to Aha moments)
- Retention and adoption widgets
- Multi-product dashboards across multiple channels
- Share dashboards -- work off same data
- Templates + drag-and-drop

**Core value hook:** "Every role sees what they care about -- CS, Product, and execs are all working from the same data."

---

### 4. Adoption Analytics
**Value:** Understand which features drive outcomes and which ones don't
- Adoption by module and feature
- Group by Feature (Golden Features concept)
- Fly filters:
  - User attributes: role, title, signup date
  - Account attributes: entitlement, industry, CS health scores
  - NPS segment comparison (promoters vs detractors)
  - In-app engagement completion
- Compare: which features correlate with positive outcomes?

**Core value hook:** "You can see exactly which features your power users adopt -- and which ones are drag. Then go move the needle on the ones that matter."

---

### 5. Funnel Analyzer
**Value:** See where users drop off on their way to value moments
- Define steps: page views, clicks, engagements
- Multi-product and cross-channel
- Drop-off rates, thru traffic, time between steps
- Trial user tracking toward conversion milestones

**Core value hook:** "Where is the critical path breaking down? Now you can see it -- and intervene."

---

### 6. Path Analyzer
**Value:** Understand organic navigation to make informed product decisions
- How users move page-to-page and within pages
- Where they get stuck, struggle, or drop out
- Start from Funnel Analyzer data to see what happens after a drop-off
- Successful path: trigger engagement for users who complete
- Close the loop: CES, effort score, in-app feedback → make changes → improve for everyone

**Core value hook:** "Stop guessing why users aren't adopting. The data tells you."

---

### 7. Retention Analytics
**Value:** Understand what actually drives users to come back
- Cohort analysis -- what behavior predicts retention?
- Blue baseline: all users as benchmark
- Filter and compare cohorts:
  - Completed a guide
  - Adopted a golden feature
  - CS health score segment
- Discover what to invest in or make more prominent

**Core value hook:** "Find your golden features -- the ones where if a user adopts them, they stick."

---

### 8. Engagements
**Value:** Deliver the right message to the right user at the right moment -- automatically
Four components:
1. **Audience**: target by user attributes, account attributes (CS health scores), in-app behavior, NPS segment, Product Mapper signals
2. **Editor**: WYSIWYG -- no dev resources
3. **Schedule**: intervals, paywalls, A/B experiments, Global Throttling (prevent overloading users)
4. **Launch**: stakeholder sign-off workflow

**Core value hook:** "Super specific and relevant. Not spray-and-pray. The right message, to the right user, at the right moment -- automated."

---

### 9. Customer Success + PX Integration
**Value:** Put product telemetry in the hands of CS without them needing to ask Product
- C360 view: product data lives alongside CS data
- Scorecards: PX data feeds health scores automatically
- Adoption Explorer
- CTAs/Cockpit: trigger playbooks from PX signals
- Journey Orchestrator: blend PX engagements with CS workflows

**Core value hook:** "Your CSMs see what users are doing in the product -- without having to ask the product team for a report."

## PX Stakeholder Map
| Stakeholder | What They Care About | Where to Focus |
|-------------|---------------------|----------------|
| VP Product | Feature adoption, roadmap prioritization | Analytics sections (4-7) |
| Head of CS | Churn signals, expansion, CSM efficiency | CS+PX integration (9), Retention (7) |
| Head of Onboarding | TTV, new user activation | Engagements (8), KCB, Dashboards (3) |
| IT/Eng | Dev resources, instrumentation overhead | Tag + Product Mapper (2) |
| VP RevOps/Growth | Trial conversion, expansion signals | Funnel Analyzer (5), Paywalls |
