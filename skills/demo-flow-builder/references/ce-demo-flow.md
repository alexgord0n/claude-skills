# Skilljar / CE Demo Flow Reference

## Core Value Proposition
Skilljar gives companies a fully brandable, multi-academy education platform that meets learners where they are, scales across products and segments, integrates deeply with CRM and CS tools, and gives CS and post-sales teams visibility into customer learning progress -- all from a single admin instance.

## Framing (use in intro)
"The LMS space has 300+ vendors. When companies choose Skilljar, it's usually because of strength across four areas: Learner Experience, Admin Experience, Commerce & Certifications, and Integrations & Analytics. That's how I'll structure today."

---

## Demo Sections (in suggested order)

### 1. Intro / Framework Set
**Value:** Set expectations, frame the demo around their priorities, not Skilljar's features
Say upfront:
- Learner Experience: branded, intuitive, persona-routed academy
- Admin Experience: one asset library, multiple academies, drag-and-drop course builder
- Commerce & Certifications: built-in, no third-party tools needed
- Integrations & Analytics: Salesforce, SSO, federated search, benchmarking

**Transition:** "We'll start in learner experience, jump into admin, then look at how Skilljar manages multiple academies by product while staying connected to your ecosystem."

---

### 2. Learner Experience
**Value:** Meet learners where they are -- branded, personalized, easy to navigate
- Fully branded UI across academies (look and feel = your company)
- Swim lanes: route learners by role, product, or audience
- Progressive learning: unlock content as prior stages complete
- Course catalog: filters that are deep linkable
  - Pre-filtered URLs for specific audiences, use cases, or campaigns
- Certifications: levels, cost (paid or free), timing, what to expect
- Federated search integration (Emailmonkey example)
  - Productized integration with CC federated search

**Core value hook:** "Learners find what they need, at the right stage of their journey -- without hunting."

**Customer examples (yellow):** Emailmonkey (fed search), Qualtrics (certification-first UX)
**Live customer references:** ClickUp, Front Academy (simple + beautiful), G2 (embedded), Omnisend (catalog), Skillskool (sidebar + stats), Hornbill (gamification)

---

### 3. Admin Experience
**Value:** One central instance managing multiple academies, products, and audiences
- Asset library: all assets centralized, filter by type, see every course they appear in, replace at source
- Course builder:
  - Curriculum with lessons + assets
  - Multiple assets per lesson
  - Actions: certificates, LinkedIn cert links
- Publish and access control:
  - Push to different domains
  - Group-based access + prerequisites
- Design: JS/CSS customization for branded polish

**Core value hook:** "One library, multiple academies. Your team isn't duplicating content -- they're publishing it everywhere from one place."

---

### 4. Commerce & Certifications
**Value:** Monetize or credential education without bolting on a third-party tool
- Built-in certification tracks and learning paths
- Paid or free access
- LinkedIn integration on certificates
- Prerequisites and gating logic
- Works across multiple domains/instances

**Core value hook:** "Certifications are a retention and expansion lever. Customers who are certified renew at higher rates."

---

### 5. Analytics
**Value:** Know what's working, benchmark against peers, tie learning to business outcomes
- Analytics dashboards with filters
- Benchmarking: how does your academy compare to similar programs?
- Course progress, user behavior, trends over time
- Salesforce integration: CS team sees learner data alongside customer health
- Federated search integration: learning data surfaced in CC and CS

**Core value hook:** "Your CS team knows which customers are engaged in education and which ones aren't -- without logging into a separate system."

---

### 6. Integrations
**Value:** Skilljar plugs into the stack you already have
- CRM: Salesforce (bidirectional)
- CS: Gainsight CS (completion data, certifications → CTAs)
- Support: Zendesk
- APIs + Webhooks
- Workflow editor
- Services and professional education monetization

---

## Multi-Instance / Multi-Product Framing
If the prospect manages education across multiple products or audiences:
- One Skilljar instance = multiple domains
- Separate product academies with distinct branding
- Central admin control
- Content shared or isolated per domain
- Analytics roll up or break out by domain

**This is a common differentiator:** Many competitors require separate instances per product.
