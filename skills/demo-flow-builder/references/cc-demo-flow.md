# Community (CC) Demo Flow Reference

## Core Value Proposition
Gainsight Customer Communities gives companies a fully brandable, flexible community platform that reduces support costs, drives product adoption, surfaces customer feedback, builds champions, and integrates deeply with your CS, support, and education stack -- all without requiring heavy technical resources to manage.

---

## Demo Sections (in suggested order)

### 1. Configurability, Customization & Personalization
**Value:** Non-technical admins can fully own and brand the community -- no dev dependency
- Two extremes in the market: OOTB (easy but rigid) vs. Custom (flexible but resource-heavy)
- Gainsight CC: tons of OOTB + custom CSS when you want it
- Theme: fonts, colors, custom CSS
- No-code customization: 18 OOTB widgets, point-and-click
- Featured Topics widget -- change content without code
- Persona-based landing pages (see Front: separate pages for end users vs. developers)

**Core value hook:** "Non-technical users administering your community. Easy to set up, easy to maintain."

**Discovery question:** "How does this compare to your current community? What aspects of the customization are resonating?"

**Customer examples (yellow):** EmailMonkey, Gong, Custom

---

### 2. Federated Search
**Value:** Help users find answers across all your content -- community, support, LMS -- in one search
- Finding the right content is hard when it's spread across community, support, and LMS
- CC federated search looks across all of these disparate systems
- Demo: Face2Face (internal demo), Gainsight community, Klaviyo
  - Klaviyo: search "advanced" -- returns results from Help Center (Zendesk) + Academy (Skilljar)
- Take it further: KCB -- accessible directly within your application
  - Reduce TTV, improve digital onboarding, reduce CS/support team load

**Core value hook:** "All your content in one place. Users find exactly what they're looking for -- wherever it lives."

**Customer examples (yellow):** Gong, Klaviyo

---

### 3. Categories / Structure
**Value:** Flexible content architecture that serves multiple products, personas, and audiences
- Community, Knowledge Base, Ideas, Product Updates
- Sub-categories with subscribe options
- Recommended + sticky topics
- Lots of flexibility to cater to different personas, products, and audiences

**Customer examples (yellow):** Docusign
**Additional references:** Gainsight, Miro, Zapier, TypeForm (multi-product)

---

### 4. Create Topics
**Value:** Reduce duplicate posts, surface best answers, build a self-service culture
- 3 types of topics
- Related topic suggestions (reduce duplicates)
- Can moderate category and tags
- AI Tags
- Best answers: question marks, checkmarks, marked at top
- 3 users can mark as best answer -- builds champions network
- Easy to see what's been answered and what hasn't

**Core value hook:** "Post deflection. Champions feel valued. Support team gets leverage."

**Customer examples (yellow):** Docusign
**Additional references:** Gainsight, Zapier

---

### 5. Groups
**Value:** Smaller, more intimate engagement for specific audiences (beta programs, advisory boards, regional groups)
- 3 types of groups (public, private, hidden)
- Hidden: product advisory boards, beta programs
- Events within groups, pinned topics
- Allow for focused engagement without noise

**Customer examples (yellow):** Veeam
**Additional references:** Freshdesk, Gainsight, Face2Face

---

### 6. Events
**Value:** Promote and market webinars and events directly in community -- no separate tool needed
- Registration handled by CC or external RSVP (Zoom, etc.)
- Host external events and link in
- Conversation threads per event

**Core value hook:** "Your community is already where your customers are -- promote events there, not in a separate system."

**Customer examples (yellow):** Veeam
**Additional references:** Freshdesk, Gainsight, Face2Face

---

### 7. Gamification
**Value:** Build champions, drive engagement, reward your most valuable community members
- Manual badges: speakers, employees, influencers
- Automatic badges: connect to CE/LMS and award on course completion
- Points system
- Leaderboards
- Profile overview showing reputation

**Core value hook:** "Build the champion network that becomes your best sales and retention asset."

**Customer examples (yellow):** Veeam
**Reference community:** Veeam leaderboard (community.veeam.com/leaderboard)

---

### 8. Ideas
**Value:** Build a product with and for your customers -- close the feedback loop visibly
- Expectations set at top of page
- Pipeline Widget: shows where ideas are in the process
- Ideas + Filters
- Feedback loop: pinned updates on delivered ideas
  - Tag users, ask questions, update status → create release note → link to KB article
- Customers feel heard -- reduces churn risk

**Core value hook:** "Build your product with your customers, not just for them. And they can see it happening."

**Customer examples (yellow):** Lucid
**Additional references:** Face2Face, Gainsight

---

### 9. Admin / Moderator Experience (if time allows)
**Value:** Full control for community admins without requiring IT or dev
- Profile moderation with filters and sharing
- Zendesk + SFDC integration
- AI-assisted article creation
- Reports and dashboards:
  - Content, User, Q&A, Audience, Engagement (SFDC), Self-Service (Zendesk), Product Feedback (SFDC)
- Email campaigns
- Salesforce integration (individual user data + reports)

---

### 10. Custom Pages (if relevant to their use case)
**Value:** Turn the community into a destination -- not just a forum
- Custom pages allow fully designed content hubs beyond standard community structure
- Use cases: AI Hub, product demos library, help center embedded in community, advocacy/legends programs, mentorship programs

**Top examples by use case:**
- **Advocacy/Champions:** Lucid Legends, Sprout Social Mentorship, Strava Superusers
- **Content Hub / Resources:** Sprout Social Library, Zapier AI Hub
- **Custom Forms / Ideation:** Airtable Product Ideas form
- **Embedded Help Center:** Tebra (help center inside community)
- **Welcome / Onboarding:** Nintex "Start Here" page
- **Product Demos Library:** KnowBe4

## CC Stakeholder Map
| Stakeholder | What They Care About | Where to Focus |
|-------------|---------------------|----------------|
| Head of Community | Engagement, content quality, champion programs | Gamification, Ideas, Groups, Custom Pages |
| Head of CS/Support | Ticket deflection, self-service | Federated Search, Create Topics, Ideas |
| VP Marketing | Brand, events, advocacy | Customization, Events, Gamification, Custom Pages |
| Product | Customer feedback, roadmap signals | Ideas, Groups (advisory boards) |
| IT/Eng | Integrations, admin burden | Admin Experience, Integrations (SFDC, Zendesk) |
